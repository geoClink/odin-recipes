Baked Eggplant Parmigiana

DESCRIPTION

For a vegetarian option with the same architectural integrity as lasagna, Eggplant Parmigiana is unbeatable. This dish trades pasta for thin, breaded slices of eggplant that are fried or baked until tender, then layered with vibrant tomato sauce and puddles of melted cheese.

To ensure the dish isn't watery, it is vital to "sweat" the eggplant slices with salt before cooking. This draws out moisture and allows the vegetable to absorb the flavors of the basil and garlic, resulting in a stack of flavors that is decadent yet surprisingly light.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 large Eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 3 cups Marinara sauce
  • 2 cups Panko breadcrumbs
  • 3 Large eggs, beaten
  • 1 lb Fresh mozzarella, sliced or shredded
  • 3/4 cup Grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup All-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp Italian seasoning
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

STEPS TO COMPLETE THE DISH

  1. Salt the eggplant rounds and let sit for 20 minutes, then pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Dredge each slice in flour, dip in beaten eggs, and coat thoroughly with seasoned breadcrumbs.
  3. Bake or pan-fry the eggplant slices until they are golden brown and tender.
  4. Layer a small amount of marinara sauce in the bottom of a deep baking dish.
  5. Arrange a layer of eggplant, followed by sauce, mozzarella, and a dusting of Parmesan.
  6. Repeat the layers, ending with a generous topping of cheese.
  7. Bake at 375°F for 30 minutes until the sauce is bubbling and the cheese is melted.
